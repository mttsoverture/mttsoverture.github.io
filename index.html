About the Programme

What is MTTS Overture?

To further the reach of MTTS training to a larger audience of deserving students, the "MTTS Overture" programme was introduced in 2023. Two-day workshops are held in colleges across different parts of India, training approximately fifty undergraduate students per camp.

Two experienced MTTS faculty conduct each camp, running two short courses from Real Analysis, Linear Algebra, Foundations, Algebra, and more — exposing students to the MTTS learning methodology and resources for self-learning.

🗓 Camps run July– December each academic year (If needed January, February). Proposals must be submitted by 15th June.

The MTTS Trust bears the cost of resource persons. Host institutes arrange food and refreshments, and may charge a nominal fee from participants. See the possibility of accommodation to resource persons. Local coordinators of a few of the earlier camps contacted alumni/local people for funding.

Hosting a Camp

How to Apply

Any college or institution in India can apply, preferably those serving students from geographically disadvantaged locations.

01
Check Eligibility
A faculty member submits the proposal with consent of their department and college authorities. Preference to colleges in geographically disadvantaged areas.
02
Submit by May
Proposals for camps (July–December) must be submitted by May via the MTTS online portal.
03
Coordinate Dates
Finalize dates with assigned MTTS faculty, keeping student and faculty availability in mind. Advertise to nearby colleges.
04
Arrange Logistics
Select 40–60 students (at least 50% from other colleges). Arrange classroom, food, and accommodation for outstation faculty and students if needed.
05
Run the Camp
MTTS provides two experienced faculty. Ensure 7 lecture sessions + 2 discussion sessions over two days run smoothly.
06
Submit Feedback
Collect participant feedback via Google Form. Submit the Local Coordinator feedback form to overture.mtts@gmail.com within 2–3 days.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from local coordinators interested in hosting an MTTS Overture programme.

What is the MTTS Overture programme?
A two-day mathematics programme for undergraduate students, designed to introduce the MTTS learning methodology and foster a love for mathematics. It features short courses on foundational topics like Analysis, Algebra, or Foundations — mostly for second-year B.Sc students with mathematics as one subject.
Who can apply to host this programme?
Any college or institution in India can apply, preferably those serving students from geographically disadvantaged locations. A faculty member must submit the proposal with the consent of their department and college authorities.
How and when should one apply?
Proposals for the academic year (July–December) should be submitted by May via the online portal at 4dspace.mtts.org.in.
What does MTTS Trust provide?
Two experienced MTTS faculty as resource persons (one acting as academic coordinator), honorarium, travel and accommodation for resource persons. Normally, lunch expenses are not supported. However, in special cases—such as when the institute is in a remote location—a contribution of up to Rs. 5,000 may be provided.
Are there any costs to the host institute?
MTTS Trust covers faculty costs. The host institute arranges food and refreshments, venue (a classroom with blackboard — no projector needed), and accommodation for outstation students if needed. As a general principle, no fee should be charged to students; however, a nominal fee may be considered in exceptional cases where necessary.
See the possibility of accommodation to resource persons. Local coordinators of a few of the earlier camps contacted alumni/local people for funding.
Since this program is typically funded through donations received by MTTS, we request the host institution to extend reasonable support wherever possible.
How are students selected?
Invite motivated undergraduates interested in mathematics. At least 50% must be from colleges other than the host institution. Prioritize students from nearby colleges, and aim for a mix of gender and backgrounds.
A Suggestion: Please share the registration form with a few faculty members from nearby colleges/institutes. The form is intended only for faculty members and should not be circulated directly to students. Faculty members are requested to nominate 2–4 students who are certain to participate in the program by filling (faculty only) in their details in the Google Form . This will help us estimate participation more accurately.
What is the schedule of the program?
Day 1: Registration begins at 8:30 AM. If there is an inaugural session, it will be kept brief. Classes will begin immediately after the inaugural session. The program will conclude at 5:30 PM.

Students traveling from very long distances may leave a little early on Day 1 with prior permission from the local coordinator.

Day 2: The program starts at 9:00 AM and continues until 5:00 PM.

The exact detailed schedule will be shared during the first session.
Are certificates issued to participants?
MTTS will not issue certificates. The host college may issue its own certificates to those who attended both days, with the Coordinator and Principal/Academic Coordinator as signatories. MTTS Trust discourages students who attend only for certificates.
How is feedback collected?
Feedback is collected via Google Form at the end of the programme. The Local Coordinator fills a separate feedback form and sends it to overture.mtts@gmail.com within 2–3 days. Hardcopy forms are available for students who cannot use Google Forms.
