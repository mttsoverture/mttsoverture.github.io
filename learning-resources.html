Immersive Learning Experiences

Summer Schools & Research Programmes

Summer schools and research internship programmes offer undergraduate students a chance to experience mathematics research, interact with leading mathematicians, and build their academic network. Most programmes provide travel, accommodation, and a stipend.

MTTS Annual Summer Camp
Mathematics Training and Talent Search Programme · Various IITs/IISERs

The flagship MTTS Summer Camp is a 3–4 week intensive residential camp held every May–June at multiple centres across India. Designed for B.Sc. students, it offers courses in Algebra, Analysis, Topology, and more — following the distinctive MTTS inquiry-based methodology. Fully residential with travel and board support.

3–4 weeks · May–JuneB.Sc. 1st–3rd YearFully Residential
MTTS Programme →
TIFR Summer Programme
Tata Institute of Fundamental Research, Mumbai

TIFR conducts annual summer programmes introducing talented students to research in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, CS, and Astronomy. The School of Mathematics at TIFR offers research internships for students to work with faculty. Stipend, travel, and accommodation are typically provided.

B.Sc./M.Sc. studentsMay–JulyStipend + Travel

ICTS Summer Research Programme
International Centre for Theoretical Sciences, Bengaluru

ICTS-TIFR offers a summer research programme for UG and PG students in mathematics. ICTS also hosts the Summer School for Women in Mathematics and Statistics — a two-week residential school for first-year B.Sc./B.Tech. women students, covering problem-solving skills in mathematics and statistics. Travel, board, and a performance-based book stipend are provided.

UG & PG studentsWomen's school availableTravel + Board supported

Harish-Chandra Research Institute (HRI), Prayagraj

HRI's SPIM is designed to introduce Master's-level mathematics to highly motivated undergraduates and first-year postgraduates. The programme features intensive lectures on Algebra, Analysis, and related areas — very similar in spirit to MTTS camps but focused on slightly more advanced material.

Institute of Mathematical Sciences, Chennai

IMSc selects about 25 students across disciplines annually for 6–8 week summer research visits (May–July). Extended 4–6 month visits (Jan–Apr or Aug–Nov) are also possible. Students work directly with IMSc faculty on research problems in mathematics, theoretical computer science, or physics.

They cover topics from Algebra, Differential Geometry, Number Theory, Topology, and more at an advanced level. Accommodation and travel support are typically provided.

Financial Support for Mathematicians

NBHM Scholarships & Fellowship

The National Board for Higher Mathematics (NBHM), under the Department of Atomic Energy, Government of India, offers prestigious scholarships to outstanding mathematics students at both the Master's and Doctoral levels. The same national written test (held in January each year) serves both schemes.

🎓
NBHM Master's Scholarship
National Board for Higher Mathematics · Dept. of Atomic Energy, Govt. of India
₹8,000
Per month stipend
2 years
Maximum tenure

Eligibility: Indian nationals enrolled or enrolling in the first year of an M.Sc. in Mathematics or Statistics at a recognised full-time programme. Bachelor's degree in Mathematics or related field required. Part-time or correspondence courses are not eligible.

Key rule: Each person can receive this scholarship only once. If already in 2nd year of M.Sc. during the award year, tenure is limited to 1 year.

Written Test in January Interview (after shortlisting) No prescribed syllabus No application fee

Bonus: NBHM test scores are also used for M.Sc./Ph.D./Integrated Ph.D. admissions at IISER Pune, HRI, IMSc, KSoM, NISER and other premier institutes — so appearing in this test opens multiple doors even beyond the scholarship itself.

National Board for Higher Mathematics · Dept. of Atomic Energy, Govt. of India

Per month (first 2 years)

Eligibility: Indian nationals with an M.Sc. in Mathematics, Physics, or Statistics, enrolled or planning to enrol in a Ph.D. in any branch of mathematics (including mathematical statistics and mathematical physics) at any recognised Indian university or research institution.

Duration: Up to 5 years total (initially 1 year, renewable on satisfactory progress). Cannot hold other major fellowships simultaneously.

Scope of research: Any area of mathematics — pure, applied, or mathematical physics — is eligible. The tenable institution must be an Indian university or research institution outside the DAE-aided institutes.

Same written test as Master's (January) Valid at any Indian university Up to 5 years tenure
NBHM Website →
💡 About the NBHM Test: There is no prescribed syllabus — questions are drawn from standard undergraduate and early postgraduate mathematics. Previous year papers with answer keys are freely available on the NBHM portal. The test serves both the Master's and Doctoral scholarship programmes, and a single application can opt for both. Candidates are shortlisted based on test scores and then called for an online interview. The test is held on a Saturday in late January each year; applications open in early December.

Higher Education Pathways

M.Sc. Admission Examinations

For undergraduate mathematics students looking to pursue postgraduate education, these national-level entrance examinations are your primary gateways. Each exam opens doors to different sets of prestigious institutions.

🔬
IIT JAM — Joint Admission Test for M.Sc.
Conducted by IITs on a rotational basis · Computer-Based Test

IIT JAM is the premier national entrance exam for admission to M.Sc., M.Sc.–Ph.D. Dual Degree, and Integrated Ph.D. programmes at all IITs and IISc. It is conducted once a year, usually in February, and is the most sought-after exam for mathematics postgraduates. About 13,000–14,000 candidates appear in the Mathematics paper annually.

Conducting Body
IITs (rotational)
Exam Mode
Computer-Based (CBT)

No. of Attempts
Unlimited
All 21 IITs IISc Bangalore NITs via CCMN M.Sc. · M.Sc.–Ph.D. Dual · Integrated Ph.D.

Eligibility: Bachelor's degree in any discipline from a recognised university. At least 55% aggregate (50% for SC/ST/PwD). No age limit. No limit on number of attempts.

Paper pattern: MCQ + MSQ (Multiple Select) + NAT (Numerical Answer Type). Negative marking only for MCQs. Duration: 3 hours.

Mathematics syllabus covers: Real Analysis (sequences, continuity, integration), Linear Algebra, Group Theory, Differential Equations, Vector Calculus, Complex Analysis basics.

Tip for MTTS students: The MTTS Overture covers Analysis and Algebra — both are core JAM topics. The MTTS approach of understanding from first principles is exactly what JAM tests. Practise previous year papers from the official JAM portal.

CUET-PG — Common University Entrance Test (Postgraduate)
Conducted by NTA · Gateway to Central and State Universities

CUET-PG is conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) and serves as the common entrance for postgraduate programmes (including M.Sc. Mathematics) at Central Universities, State Universities, and many other participating institutions across India. Around 2.5 lakh candidates appear for M.Sc. courses through CUET-PG annually. This is the route into most central universities if you are not targeting IITs.

Eligibility: A Bachelor's degree in Mathematics (or related field) from a recognised university. Minimum 50% aggregate (45% for SC/ST) for most universities.

Important: Unlike JAM, each university runs its own counselling and merit list after results. Apply to the CUET-PG exam first, then apply separately to each university you wish to join during their counselling window.

CMI Entrance Exam — Chennai Mathematical Institute
One of India's finest mathematics institutions · Pen & Paper

CMI is one of India's finest institutions for mathematics, with strong international connections. Admission is through CMI's own entrance exam — known for its emphasis on deep mathematical thinking and proof-writing, not rote learning. CMI alumni regularly go on to top global universities including MIT, Princeton, Stanford, Harvard, and top Indian institutes like IITs, IISc, IMSc, and TIFR.

Exam Pattern: Part A — 10 objective questions (40 marks, used for screening); Part B — 6 long-answer/proof-based questions (80 marks). Emphasis on logical reasoning and mathematical creativity.

Eligibility: Bachelor's degree (BA, B.Sc., B.Math., B.Stat., B.E., B.Tech.) from a recognised institution with Mathematics. INMO / National Olympiad awardees may get direct admission.

Tip: CMI tests the kind of mathematical understanding cultivated in MTTS camps — not computation but insight. Study Real Analysis, Abstract Algebra, Linear Algebra, Combinatorics, and Number Theory from first principles.

ISI Admission Test — Indian Statistical Institute
Premier institute for Mathematics & Statistics · Offline exam + Interview

ISI is one of India's most prestigious institutions for mathematical and statistical sciences, with campuses in Kolkata, Delhi, Bengaluru, Chennai, and Tezpur. The M.Math (Master of Mathematics) programme is extremely rigorous and a direct path to research careers. Admitted students receive a monthly stipend.

📍 Typical M.Sc. Preparation Timeline (for 2nd / 3rd year B.Sc. students)

1
2nd Year B.Sc.
Attend MTTS Overture or InitMath. Build strong foundation in Analysis & Algebra. Study Kumaresan's expository articles.
2
3rd Year (Sep–Oct)
Register for JAM (Feb exam). Register for NBHM test (Jan exam). Apply for summer programmes at TIFR, IMSc, HRI.
3
Final Year (Jan–May)
Write NBHM test (Jan). Write JAM (Feb). Apply for CMI & ISI (Mar–Apr). Appear for CUET-PG (Mar–Apr). Write IISER campus tests (Apr).
4
After Results (Jun–Jul)
Accept M.Sc. offer. Also consider integrated Ph.D. at HRI / IMSc / IISERs / TIFR if interested in research directly.
